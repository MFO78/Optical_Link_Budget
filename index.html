Mathematical Foundations

1. Slant Range (Z)

Exact line-of-sight distance accounting for Earth's curvature ($R_E \approx 6378$ km) and altitude ($h$).

$$Z = -R_E \sin(\theta) + \sqrt{(R_E \sin(\theta))^2 + 2R_E h + h^2}$$

2. Divergence & Spot Size

Diffraction-limited angular spread. The displayed metric shows spot size at Zenith ($h$), while actual geometric loss uses the slant range ($Z$).

$$\theta_{div} \approx 1.22 \frac{\lambda}{D_{tx}} \quad \Rightarrow \quad d_{spot\_zenith} = D_{tx} + h \cdot \theta_{div}$$

3. Geometrical Loss

Attenuation due to the expansion of the optical beam exceeding the receiver aperture area using actual slant range ($Z$).

$$L_{geo[dB]} = 20 \log_{10}\left(\frac{D_{rx}}{D_{tx} + Z\theta_{div}}\right)$$

4. Atmospheric Extinction (Satellite Downlink)

Attenuation uses the Kruse model. Because the atmosphere thins exponentially with altitude, we use the Effective Thickness ($H_{eff}$) based on the atmospheric scale height ($H_{scale} \approx 3.0$ km) and elevation angle ($E$).

$$\alpha_{dB/km} = \frac{17}{V} \left(\frac{550}{\lambda_{nm}}\right)^q \quad \Rightarrow \quad H_{eff} = \frac{H_{scale}}{\sin(E)}$$ $$L_{atm[dB]} = \alpha \cdot H_{eff}$$

5. Link Equation

Total received power ($P_{rx}$) calculated purely via power and transmission losses.

$$P_{rx[dBm]} = P_{tx[dBm]} - L_{geo} - L_{atm} - L_{point} - L_{tx\_optics} - L_{rx\_optics}$$

6. Quantum Limit Data Rate

Theoretical maximum data rate based on photon energy ($E_\gamma$) and required photons/bit ($N_{ppb}$).

$$E_{\gamma} = \frac{hc}{\lambda} \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_{bps} = \frac{P_{rx[W]}}{N_{ppb} \cdot E_{\gamma}}$$
