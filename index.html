Mathematical Foundations

1. Slant Range (Z)

Exact line-of-sight distance accounting for Earth's curvature ($R_E \approx 6378$ km) and altitude ($h$).

$$Z = -R_E \sin(\theta) + \sqrt{(R_E \sin(\theta))^2 + 2R_E h + h^2}$$

2. Divergence & Spot Size

Diffraction-limited angular spread for a uniform circular aperture.

$$\theta_{div} \approx 1.22 \frac{\lambda}{D_{tx}} \quad \Rightarrow \quad d_{spot} = Z \cdot \theta_{div}$$

3. Free Space Path Loss (FSPL) & Gains

Standard far-field formulation applied to optical frequencies.

$$G = \left(\frac{\pi D}{\lambda}\right)^2 \quad \text{and} \quad \text{FSPL}_{dB} = 20 \log_{10}\left(\frac{4 \pi Z}{\lambda}\right)$$

4. Link Equation

Total received power ($P_{rx}$) including systematic losses and transceiver efficiencies.

$$P_{rx[dBm]} = P_{tx[dBm]} + G_{tx} + G_{rx} - \text{FSPL} - L_{atm} - L_{point} + \eta_{tx} + \eta_{rx}$$

5. Quantum Limit Data Rate

Theoretical maximum data rate based on photon energy ($E_\gamma$) and required photons/bit ($N_{ppb}$).

$$E_{\gamma} = \frac{hc}{\lambda} \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_{bps} = \frac{P_{rx[W]}}{N_{ppb} \cdot E_{\gamma}}$$
